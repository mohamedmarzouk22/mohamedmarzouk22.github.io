About Me

Welcome to Mohamed marzouk’s “ モハメド　マルゾーク” website.

My scientific journey began in 2005 when I enrolled at the Faculty of Science, Ain Shams University in Cairo, Egypt. In my first year, I explored the fundamentals of chemistry, physics, mathematics, and biology. That same year, losing my grandfather to cancer shaped my ambition to pursue biophysics, hoping to contribute to cancer research. In 2009, I graduated as the top student in both biophysics and the physics department, with my graduation project focused on Intensity-Modulated Radiation Therapy ( IMRT), an advanced cancer treatment technique.

In 2010, I began my academic career as a demonstrator in the biophysics group at Ain Shams University, assisting students in laboratory work and physics assignments. During my M.Sc., I learned molecular genetics techniques, which deepened my understanding of DNA and protein dynamics. Realizing the limitations of experimental research, I turned to computational biology, believing it held greater potential to impact cancer research. This decision led me to pursue computational molecular dynamics for my Ph.D..

In 2019, I received the prestigious Egypt-Japan Education Partnership ( EJEP) scholarship, allowing me to pursue my Ph.D. at School of Life Sciences and Technology , Tokyo Institute of Technology . My research focused on protein-DNA and protein-ligand binding/unbinding dynamics using Parallel Cascade Selection Molecular Dynamics ( PaCS-MD) combined with the Markov State Model ( MSM), enabling me to explore complex biomolecular interactions.

After completing my Ph.D. in 2022, I joined the Molecular Modeling and Simulation ( MMS) Team at the National Institute of Quantum Science and Technology ( QST) in Chiba, Japan. My research focused on predicting protein stability and mutation effects, essential for drug design, using enhanced sampling techniques like Replica Exchange with Solute Tempering ( REST) and Free-Energy Perturbation ( FEP) to predict the stability indicator free energy difference (∆∆G).

In 2024, I joined Shinobu Lab at WPI-PRIMe , Osaka University, where I am currently working on designing therapeutic peptides, investigating the stability of the IGF1/IGFBP3/ALS ternary complex, and optimizing enhanced sampling techniques for protein-DNA interactions.

This journey has been driven by my unwavering commitment to advancing cancer research through computational biology. As I continue to explore the intricate dynamics of biomolecules, I am inspired to push the boundaries of what’s possible, contributing to the scientific community and, ultimately, to better therapeutic solutions for patients worldwide.

Outside of academia, moving to Osaka, the heart of the Kansai area, has been an exciting experience for my family and me. We love exploring local attractions like Kobe Suma Sea World, Kobe Oji Zoo, Banpaku Park, and Tennoji Zoo, which my kids absolutely enjoy. I am passionate about sports and active living—I enjoy swimming on weekends and am learning tennis with the hope of becoming skilled someday. I also play football once a week with my Japanese friends in Osaka, which has been a fantastic way to stay connected and active.
